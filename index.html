    About Me

    I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Cincinnati, specializing in Graph Mining and Machine Learning. My research focuses on developing advanced methods for community detection and clustering in complex graphs, such as directed and signed networks, to facilitate the identification of meaningful communities in these intricate structures. I also hold a Master's degree in Computer Science from New Jersey Institute Of Technology. I have industry experience as a Machine Learning Technology intern at Fidelity in Covington, Kentucky, and as a Software Developer intern at ADP in Roseland, New Jersey. I also served as a Technology Analyst at Morgan Stanley, New York. Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at the UC Simulation Center, collaborating with Procter & Gamble since 2020. Prior to this, I was a Software Engineer at ATOS in India. I have a keen interest in the arts, music, fashion, and history. I love to travel and make it a point to visit at least one new place whenever my hectic schedule allows. I've created a TODO list of tourist spots and activities from around the world that I want to experience. Historical places are always my top priority. One day, I hope to complete a world tour and check off everything on my list. Additionally, I enjoy sports, with badminton being my favorite game.
